These step-by-step instructions guide you through the Sketch Map Tool and provide useful recommendations.

Answers to the frequently asked questions can be found in the FAQ section below.

Step-by-step instructions

1
Create paper maps to collect data
1.1 Define your Area of Interest

Select your area of interest either by zooming into the map preview or with the search function in the sidebar.

Choose from different paper formats and a suitable page orientation. The dashed red line represents the boundaries of your area of interest, which will be printed on the Sketch Map.

Recommendations:
  • Adapt the extstrongent of your selected area to your use case. The following questions should be kept in mind:
    • What should be mapped by the participants?
    • What should be visible on the map and in what level of detail?
    • Which areas should the map cover?
  • Adapt the area extent to the paper size you want to use.
  • The area is too big? Create multiple Sketch Maps.
  • Aspects you might want to consider for the selection of the area of interest:
    • Ensure orientation on the map: streets (and their names) and some points of interest (POIs) should be visible.
    • Cover all relevstrongant areas for your use case: e.g. if studying flooding, cover especially those areas near rivers, creeks and sinkholes as well as critical infrastructure which might be affected by the flood event.
1.2 Map Quality Check

The generated Sketch Map uses a base map layer of OpenStreetMap (OSM). The Map Quality Check helps to evaluate the fitness of the area of interest for the purpose of participatory mapping based on a quality analysis of the OpenStreetMap data with the HeiGIT ohsome quality analyst - OQT. The fitness report can be downloaded as PDF file by clicking on the blue button. It includes an evaluation of the suitability of the local OSM data and recommendations for the subsequent field data collection.

Recommendations:
  • Before you use these OSM based maps of your area of interest for participatory mapping, please have a look at the analysis of the Map Quality Check to ensure that participants can orientate properly on the paper maps.
  • The analyses are focused on the Point of Interest (POI) density, the last-edit of amenities and streets, the used sources, as well as the saturation of amenity and the road network mapping. You can learn more about the analysis and each indicator in the generated PDF report.
  • The report includes useful recommendations to ensure that the generated maps are suitable for participatory mapping.

  • In case the OSM data of your area of interest is not sufficient for the purpose of participatory mapping, you can consult the Missing Maps project (ADD HYPERLINK) to improve the OSM data before using the Sketch Map Tool.
1.3 Export Sketch Map as PDF

The generated Sketch Map for your selected area of interest can be downloaded as PDF file by clicking on the blue button.

THEN:  Data collection in the field

At the moment the Sketch Map Tool detects red, blue and black markings. The tool does not differentiate between blue and black marking and combines these to one output result. The detection works best if the marked areas are filled out entirely. The tool cannot detect overlapping markings.

Recommendations:
  • Use thick felt-tip pens with intense colours.
  • If you have many participants or you want participants to provide a lot of different information, it is recommended to use individual Sketch Maps for each purpose/topic or per participant.
  • Prepare the same set of pens for all participants/groups for better comparison of the results.
  • If you collect additional information from the participants with other data collection methods such as Kobo Collect or ODK, add a number on the Sketch Map and save this number in the data collection questionnaire.
2
Paper to Geodata: Automatically digitize collected data
2.1 Scan or photograph your marked Sketch Maps
{# TODO: check max file size#}

After the field data collection, scan or photograph the marked Sketch Maps. The colour detection works best when scanning the Sketch Maps. When taking a picture instead, make sure that the QR-Code is complete and that all globes are visible on the image. The upload works for Jpg or png files and the files may not be larger than 50 MB.

Recommendations:
  • If you cannot scan, the photos must be taken from above in a 180-degree angle (parallel above the sketch map) to produce the best possible result.
  • Please upload a complete image of the sketch map and do not try to cut out the map from the photo.
  • Take care of the lighting conditions when taking a picture.
2.2 Upload your Sketch Maps

Scan or photograph your marked Sketch Maps after data collection. Then, upload one or more marked Sketch Maps at once.

  • It is also possible to upload more than one sketch map at the same time.
  • If you are experiencing problems during the upload process, check out the FAQ section below.
2.3 Download results as Geodata

Download the sketches in different geodata formats (Geotiffs, Vector data or All-In-One-QGIS projects).

  • You can download the georeferenced Sketch Map as a GeoTIFF or the colour-detected shapefiles (for both red and blue/black markings) in one ZIP-folder.
FINALLY: Import Geodata in QGIS
  • You can open the colour-detected shapefiles and the further analyses directly in a QGIS project.
  • When you open the QGIS project it should look similar to this example and show all the markings on your sketch maps. If anything looks strange, please check your uploaded sketch maps and if necessary, scan them again or take new pictures.
  • Opened as a new QGIS project the automatic colour-detected markings on the sketch map can be further analysed. One analysis is the count of overlapping areas between the markings. The analysis should work automatically within the sketch map tool but if problems occur the analysis is a combination of the union and count-duplicates scripts → check Screenshots of Analysis tools (combined)
  • To finally see the analysed result of the count duplicates you need to adapt the symbology to your needs and the number of analysed sketch maps!
